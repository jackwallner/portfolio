Streak Finder

Mines four-hundred days of Apple Health history to surface the fitness streaks you've already built — and protects them with a 7 PM at-risk nudge.

View source ↗

§ The pitch

People underestimate their own consistency. You probably have a fifty-day streak of seven-thousand-step days going right now, and your phone simply doesn't tell you. Streak Finder reads your Apple Health history, evaluates every metric at every threshold tier on both daily and weekly cadence, and surfaces the longest active run as a hero streak — with the rest as tappable badges and a calendar heatmap of every hit and miss in the last year.

Then it nudges you, exactly once, at 7 PM, if today's hero streak isn't locked in yet.

§ The discovery engine

This is the core of the project. Most "streak" apps make you commit to a goal up front and then track adherence. Streak Finder does the inverse: it computes adherence first and infers your goal.

  1. Pull history. Sum-per-day for quantity types; workout counts for HKWorkout; sleep attributed to the day each asleep sample ended on. ~400 days back.
  2. Evaluate. For each of nine metrics, score every threshold tier — for steps that's 3k / 5k / 7.5k / 10k / 12.5k / 15k — at both daily and weekly cadence.
  3. Pick. The highest threshold per (metric, cadence) with current ≥ minLength: 3 days for daily, 2 weeks for weekly.
  4. Rank. score = length × metricWeight × tierBonus. The top one becomes the hero. The rest are badges.
  5. Don't break it prematurely. Today (or this week) doesn't end the streak until the day ends — if you've already crossed the threshold, the live counter shows it.

The clever bit is the daily-with-weekly-fallback rule: if your 7-day step streak breaks because you took a rest day, your weekly step streak is unaffected. The momentum survives.

It tells you a thing about your past that you're already proud of, instead of a thing about your future that you're guilty about.

§ Why Swift 6 strict concurrency

This app has a hot path that touches HealthKit, SwiftData, an App Group UserDefaults snapshot, and four UI surfaces (phone, watch, two widgets) — all of which can read at any time. Strict concurrency wasn't optional, it was a forcing function. HealthKitService is the only HK toucher; StreakStore is @MainActor; SnapshotStore serialises the top-N streaks to App Group defaults so widgets render immediately without their own HK round-trip.

The result is widgets that show the right number on first paint, every time, with no stale-frame flicker.
